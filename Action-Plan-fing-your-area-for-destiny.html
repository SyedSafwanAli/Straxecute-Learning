Welcome to Building a Winning Career

Action Plan: Find Your Area of Destiny

In the Quick Wins section you resolved to take control of your career. Congratulations, you’re now officially in the driver’s seat. Now, where are you headed?
To help you create a high-level vision of what you want your future to hold, think through your answers to these questions:

  • What will I be doing?
  • Where will I be working?
  • What part of the world do I want to live in?
  • Will I be managing a team?
  • How will I have changed?
  • What will I be learning about?

What Drives You?

When you answer these questions, you’ll likely find some trends in your answers.

  • Are you talking about things that you would consider your passions?
  • Are you thinking of ideas that get you excited about work? • Do your answers make you feel happy?

Jack believes that mission and values drive the success of an organization by getting people focused on the tasks and behaviors that matter most.

Think about how you can turn the things that matter most to you – things that make you passionate, excited, and happy – into your own mission and values.

Why It Matters

  • Because you spend too much time at work to settle for a job that doesn’t fulfill you. You deserve more than that – and you can get it if you are willing to try.
  • Because you have to take a proactive approach to building your career. You can’t count on others to manage it for you.
  • Because you need to have a goal in mind. Even though it might evolve and change as you grow, having something to work toward is a crucial element in building a winning career.

Jack talks about finding the “area of destiny” – a career that marries the things you do best with the things you love to do.

By envisioning your future and identifying themes of what makes you passionate, excited, and happy, you’re beginning to figure out what types of work you love to do.

The other half of the equation is assessing the things that you do best – the skills that you can offer that no one else can, or that you (and others) think you excel at.

Imagine these two lines of skills and passions intersecting at a “sweet spot.” Can you think of a career that would fall within that area of destiny?

There is a worksheet in the Game Pages in the Playbook to help you find your area of destiny.
